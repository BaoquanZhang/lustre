DESC
kexec loads a bootable image into kernel space so that the kernel can
reload itself by jumping to this image rather than going back through
the bios and boot loader.  lkcd/mcore uses this facility to bring the 
kernel back to a known state before trying to write a dump to disk.

search for the patch name 'kexec' at 

	http://www.osdl.org/cgi-bin/plm?module=search

for recent versions that andyp may have generated.  also see, 
	
	http://lwn.net/Articles/22085/

for how its used by the 'soft reboot' lkcd dump method that is based on the
2.4 mcore dump method.
EDESC
